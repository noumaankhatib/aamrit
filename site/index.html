Welcome to Yeskay Mango Farms.

Founded in 2008, our company has grown from a small family farm into one of Maharashtra's leading names in the Alphonso mango industry. Leveraging western Maharashtra's ideal climate and rich red soil, we specialize in cultivating the world-renowned Alphonso — celebrated for its exquisite flavor, vibrant color, and unmatched sweetness.

We combine time-honored farming techniques with modern practices to ensure our mangoes meet the highest standards. Our dedicated team works tirelessly to maintain the health and productivity of our orchards, employing sustainable methods to preserve the environment while delivering superior quality fruit.

We are passionate about bringing the best Alphonso mangoes to your table — through direct sales, partnerships, or export. Our commitment to excellence and customer satisfaction drives us to continually enhance our practices and expand our reach.

Our Process

From flowering to your kitchen, by hand.

Four steps. Done the slow way. Three generations of Konkani know-how.

  1. 01

    Cultivation

    Year-round care across 402+ acres. Mineral-rich red lateritic soil, drip irrigation, organic mulching, and hand-pruning by our team of farmers.

  2. 02

    Handpicked

    Each mango harvested at the precise moment of maturity. No machines, no nets, no bruising — only experienced hands and shoulder-baskets.

  3. 03

    Natural Ripening

    Cushioned in straw-lined wooden crates and ripened slowly the traditional Konkani way — never with calcium carbide, ethylene gas, or any chemical agent.

  4. 04

    Delivered Fresh

    Picked, packed, and dispatched within 24 hours from farm to your nearest hub. Cold-chain partners deliver pan-India in 2–4 days.

The No-Carbide Promise

Why ours taste different.

Most Indian mangoes are force-ripened with calcium carbide — a chemical that ripens fruit overnight but kills its flavour, aroma and natural shine. It's also unhealthy.

We refuse to use it. Our mangoes ripen in straw-lined crates over 5–7 days, the way they have for a hundred years in the Konkan. The difference is something you'll taste in the first bite.

What we do
  • 5–7 day straw-cradle ripening
  • Organic compost & mulching
  • Drip irrigation, no flooding
  • Hand-sort, hand-pack
What we never do
  • Calcium carbide (banned but common)
  • Ethylene gas chambers
  • Synthetic colour washes
  • Buying from middlemen

Common Questions

Honest answers, in plain English.

How do I know your mangoes are really naturally ripened?

Three things: (1) we own every farm — there's no point in our chain where a middleman could swap fruit; (2) you can visit any of our 10 plots in Ratnagiri or Raigad at any time during the season; (3) artificially ripened Alphonso looks uniformly yellow with no aroma — ours ripen unevenly with a strong floral smell, which is the natural sign.

When is the season and how do I pre-order?

Alphonso is a once-a-year fruit. Pre-orders open every March; deliveries run from late March through early June, with peak fruit between mid-April and mid-May. Reserve early — we sell out most years by April.

What if my mangoes arrive damaged?

100% replacement, no questions asked. WhatsApp us a photo within 48 hours of delivery and we'll dispatch a fresh crate at our cost. Our 100% Satisfaction Guarantee has been part of how we do business since 2008.

Why is your Alphonso pricier than what I see in local markets?

Real Konkan Alphonso is the world's most expensive mango variety to grow. We never use chemicals, never auction-buy from middlemen, and never let an unripe fruit ship. The 5–7 day natural ripening alone costs us 30% more than gas-ripened alternatives. You're paying for actual Alphonso, hand-picked from the GI-tag belt.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes — through our sister brand Westmango Agro Export. We currently ship to UAE, Singapore, UK, USA, and Australia. For export inquiries, please use the B2B form.

Can I visit your farms?

Absolutely — we encourage it. Our Vilye and Chordhe estates welcome visits during the harvest season (April–May). Drop us a WhatsApp at least a week ahead and we'll arrange a guided tour with chai and fresh-cut mango.
